Medicare 101

Frequently asked questions about Medicare

What is Medicare?

Medicare is a federal health insurance program for people 65 and older, as well as some younger people with disabilities and those with End-Stage Renal Disease.

When should I enroll?

Your Initial Enrollment Period starts 3 months before you turn 65 and lasts for 7 months total. Missing this window can result in penalties.

How much does Medicare cost?

Most people get Part A premium-free. Part B has a monthly premium (typically around $185 in 2025). Part C and D costs vary by plan.

What's the difference between Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage?

Original Medicare is Parts A & B managed by the government. Medicare Advantage (Part C) is offered by private companies and often includes extra benefits.

Do I need a separate drug plan?

If you have Original Medicare, yes – you need a separate Part D plan. If you choose Medicare Advantage, most plans include drug coverage.

Can I change my plan?

Yes, during the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 – December 7) each year, you can switch Medicare Advantage or Part D plans.
